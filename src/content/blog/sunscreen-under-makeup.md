# Sunscreen Under Makeup: The Right Way (No Pilling, No White Cast)

The number one reason people skip sunscreen on makeup days? It pills, it leaves a white cast, or it makes foundation slide off by noon. The good news: with the right product and the right technique, SPF and makeup get along just fine. Here's how to do it.

## The golden rule: SPF goes after skincare, before makeup

Sunscreen is the **last step of skincare**, not the first step of makeup. The order looks like this:

1. **Moisturizer** — let it absorb fully
2. **Sunscreen** — your broad-spectrum SPF
3. **Makeup** — primer, foundation, the works

The mistake most people make is rushing. If you pile foundation on top of wet sunscreen, you get pilling — those little rolls of product. The fix is patience, not a different product.

## Why pilling happens (and how to stop it)

Pilling is usually caused by **layering too fast** or **mixing incompatible textures**. To prevent it:

- Wait **60–90 seconds** after applying SPF before touching makeup
- Use a **thin, even layer** — more product is not more protection if it never sets
- **Press, don't rub** your foundation on top (a damp sponge helps)
- Avoid heavy silicone primers directly over a silicone-heavy sunscreen
