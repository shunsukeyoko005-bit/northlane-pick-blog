# Mineral vs Chemical Sunscreen: Which Should You Pick?

If you've ever stood in the sunscreen aisle wondering whether to grab "mineral" or "chemical," you're not alone. Both protect your skin — they just do it differently. Here's the simple breakdown so you can pick the right one with confidence.

## How they work

**Mineral (physical) sunscreen** uses zinc oxide and/or titanium dioxide. It sits on top of the skin and reflects and scatters UV. It starts working the moment it's applied.

**Chemical sunscreen** uses filters (like avobenzone or octinoxate) that absorb UV and convert it to heat, which is released from the skin. It tends to feel lighter and disappears more easily.

## Side-by-side

| | Mineral | Chemical |
|---|---------|----------|
| Active ingredients | Zinc oxide, titanium dioxide | Avobenzone, octinoxate, etc. |
| Feel | Thicker, can leave white cast | Lightweight, blends clear |
| Works immediately? | Yes | Wait ~15 min before sun |
| Best for | Sensitive / reactive skin | Everyday wear, under makeup |
| Downside | White cast on deeper tones | May sting sensitive eyes |

## Which should *you* pick?

**Choose mineral if you:**
- Have sensitive, reactive, or acne-prone skin
- Want protection that works the second you step outside
- Prefer fewer chemical filters

**Choose chemical if you:**
- Want a lightweight, invisible finish
- Wear it daily under makeup
- Dislike any white cast

> The honest truth: **the best sunscreen is the one you'll actually wear every day.** A "perfect" formula sitting unused protects nothing.
