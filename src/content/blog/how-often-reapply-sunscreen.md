# How Often Should You Reapply Sunscreen? (A Simple All-Day Plan)

Most people apply sunscreen once in the morning and assume they're covered all day. They're not. Sunscreen wears off — and the protection you applied at 8 a.m. is mostly gone by lunch. Here's the simple rule.

## The basic rule: every 2 hours

When you're outdoors, reapply **every two hours**. Sunscreen breaks down with sun exposure, sweat, and normal skin contact, so even a high SPF needs topping up to keep working.

## When you need to reapply sooner

- **After swimming** — reapply right after you get out and towel dry, even if it says "water resistant."
- **After heavy sweating** — sports, hiking, and hot days wear it off faster.
- **After towel-drying** — the towel physically removes product.

## "But I'm wearing makeup"

This is the #1 reason people skip reapplication — they don't want to wreck their makeup. The fix is a **stick or powder format** you can swipe over makeup without rubbing.

A balm-style stick like the [Supergoop! Glow Stick SPF 50](https://www.amazon.com/dp/B0883DRT32?tag=northlanepick-20) glides on over foundation with no mess, which makes the every-2-hours rule realistic when you're out and about.

## A simple all-day plan

| Time | What to do |
|------|-----------|
| Morning | Apply a fluid SPF as the last step of your routine |
| Every 2 hours outside | Reapply (stick over makeup is easiest) |
| After swimming/sweating | Reapply immediately after towel-drying |

## How much is enough?

For your face and neck, use about **two finger-lengths** of product. Most people apply far too little, which means you're getting a fraction of the SPF on the label.

Keep a reapplication SPF in your bag, your car, and your beach tote — the easiest sunscreen to reapply is the one you have within reach.
